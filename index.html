Bio

Hello! I'm currently a first-year PhD student at New York University. I obtained my MSc in Statistics from Renmin University of China, under the guidance of Prof. Hanfang Yang and Prof. Hongteng Xu. I was also a visiting research intern at Carnegie Mellon University, co-hosted by Prof. Shixiang (Woody) Zhu and Prof. Holly Wiberg. My research interests lie in machine learning for sequential data modeling and sequential decision making. Specifically, I focus on reinforcement learning, stochastic process, control theory, and their applications in cyber-physical energy systems.

News

I am excited to begin my PhD journey at NYU in the fall of 2025!
I am eager to explore interesting research ideas with fellow scholars and look forward to engaging in in-depth collaboration, particularly in the areas of machine learning and control, as well as data-driven optimization for sustainable power systems.
