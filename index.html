Bio

Hello! I'm currently a first-year PhD student at New York University. I obtained my MSc in Statistics from the School of Statistics, Renmin University of China, under the guidance of Prof. Hongteng Xu and Prof. Hanfang Yang. I was also a visiting research intern at Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Prof. Shixiang (Woody) Zhu and Prof. Holly Wiberg. My research interests lie in machine learning for sequential data modeling and sequential decision making. Specifically, I focus on stochastic process, control theory, and their applications in cyber-physical energy systems.

News

I am excited to begin my PhD journey at NYU in the fall of 2025!

I am eager to explore exciting research ideas with fellow scholars and look forward to engaging in in-depth collaboration, particularly in the areas of learning and optimization theory, as well as data-driven optimization for sustainable power systems.
